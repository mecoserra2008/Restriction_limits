## 1. Clarify the objective mismatch (most important point)

You say:

> *“Preparing the partial effects (partial derivatives) of the d.p. with respect to the regressors”*

But your estimation stage is:

*   **Fama–MacBeth cross‑sectional factor pricing**
*   **2SLS for endogeneity**

⚠️ These are **asset‑pricing identification tools**, not **P\&L explainers**.

### Why this matters

*   A P\&L explainer needs **explainability ex post**
*   Fama–MacBeth estimates **risk premia**, not **realized P\&L attribution**
*   The regression coefficients do **not equal partial derivatives** of value

✅ **Recommendation**  
Separate the system into **two layers**:

| Layer                     | Purpose                                   |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| P\&L Attribution          | Uses known Greeks × realized factor moves |

## 2. Your dependent variable is not well aligned

```python
log_delta_V = np.log(V).diff()
```

### Problems

1.  Log P\&L of a **portfolio value** mixes:
    *   allocation changes
    *   coupons
    *   reinvestment
2.  Log returns break the **additivity** needed for attribution:
    $$
    \Delta V \neq \sum \Delta V_i
    $$

✅ **Better (for explainability)**

At bond or position level:

```python
delta_V = V.diff()
```

Or normalized:

```python
pnl_bp = 10000 * delta_V / V.shift(1)
```

Log returns are fine later — not for attribution.

## 3. Greeks are used, but not tied to factor shocks

You compute:

```python
D, C, Z, CS
```

But you **never explicitly multiply** them by:

*   Δy (rate move)
*   Δspread
*   Δcurve nodes

This means you’re *estimating effects statistically* instead of **explaining realized P\&L**.

✅ **Correct structural P\&L identity**

For each bond $$i$$:

$$
\Delta V_i \approx
- D_i \cdot \Delta y
+ \tfrac12 C_i (\Delta y)^2
- D^{cs}_i \cdot \Delta CS_i
+ \text{Carry}_i
+ \text{Residual}
$$

Implement this **deterministically** before any regression.
