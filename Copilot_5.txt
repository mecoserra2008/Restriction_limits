What is happening
The message says (in essence):

“We can’t complete the action for PivotTable_Tipo on sheet Dashboard because there is already a PivotTable ‘PivotTable_RAF’ there. Free up space and try again.”

So:

When the macro runs and does pt.RefreshTable, Excel tries to expand one of the PivotTables (the one named PivotTable_Tipo).

That expansion would make its range overlap the range of another PivotTable on the Dashboard (PivotTable_RAF).

Excel does not allow two PivotTables to overlap, so it throws Run-time error 1004 and stops the macro.

What you should expect from this:

The macro stops at that point.

Some PivotTables may be refreshed, others not.

The dashboard ends up partially updated / inconsistent.

Why this started happening now
Now that the Data sheet is dynamic and you’re importing real data, some PivotTables (especially:

Limites por Tipo de Linha

Limites por Tipo de Contraparte

Análise RAF

are getting more rows than before (more tipos, mais contrapartes, etc.).

When they grow, they “bump into” each other if they’re placed too close on the Dashboard.

How to fix it (one-time layout fix)
You don’t need to change the macro.
You need to give each PivotTable more breathing room on the Dashboard.

1. Identify the three PivotTables
On the Dashboard sheet:

Click inside the table for Limites por Tipo de Linha → PivotTable Analyze → in the Name box you’ll see something like PivotTable_TipoLinha.

Click inside Limites por Tipo de Contraparte → that’s probably PivotTable_Tipo.

Click inside Análise RAF → that’s PivotTable_RAF.

2. Make sure they don’t overlap now or when they grow
Do this:

Leave at least 2–3 empty rows between the bottom of one PivotTable and the header of the next block.

If needed, move one PivotTable further down:

Click inside the PivotTable

Press Ctrl + A twice (to select the whole PivotTable)

Cut (Ctrl + X)

Paste (Ctrl + V) into a lower cell (e.g., move Análise RAF further down).

The key rule:

No PivotTable’s range can touch or cross another PivotTable’s range—now or when it expands.
